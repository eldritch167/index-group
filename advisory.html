Advisory & Founding Circle

Index Group is supported by governance professionals, corporate leaders, international advisors, and returned global professionals with long-term ties to Botswana.

Role of Advisors

Advisory members provide non-binding guidance on evaluation standards, methodology updates, and strategic positioning. They do not make final inclusion decisions, manage commercial operations, or hold equity in Index Group. Advisors serve as institutional checks — ensuring that Index Group maintains governance standards, avoids commercial bias, and operates with long-term thinking.

Local + International Mix

Index Group's advisory structure blends local knowledge with international expertise:

  • Local Advisors: Governance professionals, corporate leaders, and institutional experts based in Botswana
  • International Advisors: Global professionals with experience in index development, corporate benchmarking, and institutional standards
  • Diaspora & Peace Corps Alumni: Returned professionals who maintain ties to Botswana and bring external perspective

Peace Corps / Diaspora / Global Professionals Welcome

Index Group actively seeks advisory participation from individuals who have worked internationally but maintain long-term ties to Botswana. This includes:

  • Peace Corps Botswana alumni with relevant expertise
  • Batswana professionals who have worked abroad and returned
  • International experts with sustained engagement in Botswana's institutional development

Advisory ≠ Employment

Advisory roles at Index Group are non-executive and non-commercial. Advisors do not receive salaries, equity, or performance-based compensation. This structure ensures that advisory guidance is independent and focused on institutional integrity rather than commercial outcomes.

Conflict-of-Interest Protocol

All advisory members must disclose material relationships with organizations under evaluation. Advisors with direct commercial ties to entities being reviewed are recused from related deliberations.

This protocol is enforced through written declarations updated annually.
