Botswana Index

Why Botswana Needs a Formal Index

Botswana's corporate landscape has matured significantly, yet credible, consistent, and comparable recognition of excellence remains fragmented. A formal index provides:

  • Credibility — an independent, transparent benchmark that organisations can trust and reference in stakeholder communications.
  • Comparability — a standardised framework that allows meaningful evaluation across sectors, sizes, and time periods.
  • Continuity — an annual, archival record that tracks progress, identifies trends, and preserves institutional memory.

Unique Localization

The Botswana Index is deliberately localised. It is not a transplanted global ranking or imported methodology. It is built from the ground up to reflect Botswana's operating realities — its regulatory environment, cultural context, economic structure, and national development priorities — while still meeting or exceeding international governance and transparency standards.

National Narrative Positioning

The Index aligns with Botswana's Vision 2036 aspirations of becoming a high-income, knowledge-based economy with strong institutions and inclusive growth. It contributes to this narrative by:

  • Highlighting organisations that exemplify governance excellence and ethical leadership
  • Encouraging continuous improvement across the corporate sector
  • Positioning Botswana as a regional leader in institutional maturity and accountability

Importantly, this alignment is independent — the Index is not government-led, funded, or controlled. It operates at arm's length from public institutions to preserve objectivity and credibility.

Statement of Independence

Index Group and the Botswana Index maintain strict independence from political, commercial, or institutional influence. Evaluation, inclusion, and recognition decisions are based solely on methodology and evidence, never on sponsorship, relationships, or external pressure. This independence is non-negotiable and is protected through governance firewalls, conflict-of-interest declarations, and independent advisory oversight.
