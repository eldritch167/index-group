Methodology

Index 100 Methodology

This methodology outlines the rigorous, governance-first process used to evaluate and recognize organisations in the Index 100. It is designed to be transparent, repeatable, and independent.

A. Evaluation Pillars

Organizations are evaluated across four core pillars:

1. Governance & Integrity

Board composition, conflict-of-interest protocols, audit independence, regulatory compliance, and ethical conduct records.

2. Leadership & Human Capital

Executive continuity, succession planning, professional development programs, gender and diversity metrics, and talent retention indicators.

3. Innovation & Digital Readiness

Technology adoption, digital infrastructure maturity, R&D investment, intellectual property development, and process modernization.

4. Social & Institutional Impact

Community engagement, environmental responsibility, economic contribution, sectoral influence, and stakeholder value creation.

B. Data Sources

  • Public disclosures
  • Structured submissions
  • Third-party validation
  • Advisory review layers

C. Governance Firewalls

  • Separation between sponsors and evaluation
  • Independent advisory oversight
  • Conflict-of-interest declarations (mandatory)

D. Inclusion & Removal Clauses

  • Post-inclusion review triggers
  • Ethical breach clauses
  • Material misrepresentation grounds
  • Removal without financial penalty to Index Group

E. Appeals Mechanism

  • Formal appeal window
  • Independent review panel
  • Non-public deliberation
  • Final determination archived
