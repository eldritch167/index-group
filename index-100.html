Index 100

What the Index 100 Represents

The Index 100 is the flagship annual recognition of corporate excellence in Botswana. It identifies and celebrates organisations that demonstrate outstanding performance across the core pillars of governance, leadership, innovation, and social/institutional impact.

Who It Is For

Corporates

Private sector organisations demonstrating governance maturity and operational excellence.

State-Owned Enterprises (SOEs)

Public sector entities meeting institutional standards for transparency and impact.

NGOs & Civil Society

Organisations with formal governance structures and measurable social outcomes.

Institutions

Research bodies, professional associations, and knowledge platforms.

What Inclusion Signals

Inclusion in the Index 100 signals:

  • High standards of ethics and integrity
  • Mature and effective leadership
  • Demonstrated innovation capability
  • Meaningful social and institutional impact

It is a mark of institutional maturity — a credible, external validation that an organisation operates at a level of excellence that can be benchmarked both locally and internationally.

Annual Cadence & Archival Permanence

The Index 100 is published annually. Each edition is permanent and archived. Historical data is preserved to allow longitudinal tracking of progress, trends, and institutional development over time. Inclusion is not a one-off award; it is a recurring standard that organisations must continue to meet.

Future Expansion

The Index 100 is the starting point of a broader Botswana Index Series:

  • Index 400: Extended recognition platform for emerging organisations
  • Index 500: Comprehensive institutional benchmarking across sectors
  • Brand Excellence Summit: Annual convening for governance and brand leaders — partnered
  • BAID Brief: Strategic intelligence and institutional analysis — partnered

Index ≠ Competition

The Index 100 is a recognition platform, not a ranking system. There is no first place. Inclusion is the standard.
